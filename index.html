About

Dr. Shalini Mahato is currently working as an Assistant Professor in the Department of Electronics and Computer Engineering at the National Institute of Advanced Manufacturing Technology (NIAMT), Ranchi. She has done her Ph.D. in Computer Science and Engineering from Birla Institute of Technology, Mesra, and both an M. Tech and B. Tech in Computer Science and Engineering from Maulana Abul Kalam Azad University, West Bengal. Her academic and professional journey reflects a deep commitment to advancing research, innovation, and excellence in the field of technology. With a career that began as a developer at Tata Consultancy Services (TCS), she transitioned into academia to pursue her passion for research and teaching. Over the years, she have contributed to more than 30 research papers, including 7 indexed in SCI and 6 in Scopus, contributing significantly to the fields of artificial intelligence, machine learning, and computer science.

RESEARCH & INNOVATION

Driven by a desire to solve real-world problems, she led a project that won the prestigious MSME Idea Hackathon 2022, sponsored by the Ministry of Micro, Small & Medium Enterprises (MSME), Government of India. Additionally, she has filed 3 Indian patents, reflecting her commitment to innovation and practical applications of cutting-edge research.

Area of Research : Artificial Intelligence, Machine Learning, Biomedical Signal Processing

ABOUT NIAMT

Industrial development plays an important role in the development of the country. To Provide the requisite quality & quantity of trained manpower at different level, Govt. of India set up the National Institute of Foundry and Forge Technology (NIFFT) in the year 1966 in collaboration with UNDP-USESCO. NIFFT is registered as Society under the Societies Registration Act of 1860. The management of the Institute is vested with Board of Governors with Chairman at its apex and members representing Govt. of India (Ministry of HRD & industry), AICTE, Private and Public enterprises, technical and R & D organizations. Recently, the institute changed its name to National Institute of Advanced Manufacturing Technology (NIAMT). During the last five-six decades NIAMT (formerly NIFFT) has earned a reputation as a leading institute for teaching, training, research & development, consultancy and documentation and information retrieval services in manufacturing technology and related areas of academic and industrial interest. With the globalization of the market, competitiveness in industries has increased tremendously.
